# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small team of UV printing technicians (currently one, Daniel, with future coworkers to be trained). They use this tool during their workday — at their desk or standing next to a printer — often under time pressure, sometimes on a phone. They are technically competent but not software engineers. New workers must be able to use the tool without formal training.

## Product Purpose

KEYOUT is an internal repository for UV-print keyboard and device-front layout designs. It centralizes design files, printer framing settings (offset, rotation, scale), verification history, and traceability so that any trained worker can reproduce a print job correctly without depending on a single person's knowledge. Success means a new worker can find a design, read its settings, and print it correctly on their first attempt.

## Brand Personality

Confiable, ágil, profesional. The tool should feel like a well-made workshop instrument: no decoration for its own sake, everything in its place, immediately understandable. Not corporate-cold, not startup-playful.

## Anti-references

**Odoo**: Dense, cluttered UI with poor visual hierarchy, too many competing elements on screen at once, dated aesthetics, confusing navigation patterns, high cognitive load. This tool must be the opposite: focused, fast to scan, low friction.

## Design Principles

1. **One task, one screen** — each view has a clear primary action. Don't make the user look for what to do next.
2. **Fast to scan, not just fast to load** — workers arrive with a specific job. Typography hierarchy and spacing must let them find information in seconds, not minutes.
3. **Consistent over clever** — identical interactions work identically everywhere. No surprises, no learning curve for new screens.
4. **Accessible on any device** — the tool must work on a phone held next to a printer. Touch targets, readable text, no hover-only interactions.
5. **Trust through density control** — show the right amount of information at each level. Details are available but never forced on the user.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No known disability-specific needs. Mobile web use is a primary requirement — workers will access the tool from phones next to printers. All interactive elements must meet minimum touch target sizes (44×44px), text must remain legible at mobile viewport widths, and no interaction may depend on hover alone.
